The First Decision

Federal vs. provincial incorporation.

This is the first fork in the road, and it comes down to where you plan to do business. We'll ask about your growth plans and how you intend to operate, then recommend the route that fits — not the one that just happens to cost more.

Federal Incorporation

Sets up your corporation under the Canada Business Corporations Act, so you can operate in every province and carry the same name across the country. It's the right call if you expect to grow beyond Ontario.

  • ✓ Operate anywhere in Canada under one federal corporation
  • ✓ Stronger name protection nationwide
  • ✓ Recognized structure if you deal across borders
  • ✓ Extra-provincial registration in Ontario handled for you

Provincial Incorporation

Sets up your corporation under Ontario's Business Corporations Act. If your customers and operations are here in Ontario, this is usually the simpler, less expensive path — and the one most small businesses start with.

  • ✓ Lower filing and setup costs
  • ✓ Fewer ongoing filings to keep track of
  • ✓ Name protection within Ontario
  • ✓ One set of provincial rules to follow

Choosing the Right Structure

Incorporation is one of several options.

Incorporating isn't automatically the right move for everyone. Before you decide, it helps to see the three common structures side by side and weigh the trade-offs against your goals, your tax picture, and how much personal risk you're comfortable carrying.

01

Sole Proprietorship

You and the business are one and the same. It's cheap and quick to register, but there's no liability shield, and your profit is reported on your personal T1 return.

02

Partnership

Two or more owners share the business. A written partnership agreement matters, liability is generally shared, and income flows through to each partner's own return.

03

Incorporation

A separate legal entity that lives on its own. You get limited liability and real tax-planning room, in exchange for more paperwork and its own T2 corporate return.

Why business owners choose to incorporate.

Liability Protection

A corporation is its own legal person, so your home and savings generally aren't on the hook for business debts.

Professional Credibility

The "Inc." or "Ltd." after your name signals permanence, and some clients and lenders prefer to deal with a corporation.

Tax Planning

A Canadian-controlled private corporation can claim the small business deduction on its first $500,000 of active business income, plus options like income deferral.

Ownership Transfer

Because ownership lives in shares, bringing in a partner or handing the business to the next generation is far cleaner than with a proprietorship.

Business Financing

A corporation can build its own credit history and issue shares, which often makes it easier to raise money or bring investors on board.
