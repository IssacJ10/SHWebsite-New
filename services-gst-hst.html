GST/HST Registration

Set up correctly, from the start.

You have to register once your worldwide taxable sales pass $30,000 over four consecutive calendar quarters. Cross that line and you stop being a small supplier — you're expected to start charging HST right away. We watch that threshold with you so a good month doesn't turn into a surprise CRA obligation you didn't see coming.

Sometimes it pays to register before you have to. Voluntary GST HST registration lets you recover the HST on your startup and equipment costs through input tax credits, which can matter a lot in year one. We help you weigh that trade-off, then open your GST/HST program account under your business number, set the right effective date, and file the paperwork with the CRA for you.

Input Tax Credits

Claim every eligible credit — with airtight records.

Once you're registered, the HST you pay on business purchases isn't a sunk cost — you claim it back as an input tax credit and remit only the difference. Miss those credits and you overpay; over-claim and you invite a CRA review. We aim for the sweet spot: everything you're entitled to, nothing you're not.

What you can claim

Input tax credits apply to the HST you pay on goods and services used to make taxable supplies. The everyday costs of running your business usually qualify — the trick is claiming the right portion when an expense is part personal.

  • • Office supplies, software and equipment
  • • Professional services and consulting
  • • Vehicle and mileage used for business
  • • Commercial rent and facility costs

Records that hold up

A credit is only as good as the receipt behind it. The CRA can ask you to prove any input tax credit you claimed, so we keep your invoices organised and tied to each return — so an audit is a quick answer, not a scramble.

  • • Invoices retained and matched to returns
  • • Supplier's GST/HST number captured on file
  • • Meals, entertainment and blocked items handled correctly
