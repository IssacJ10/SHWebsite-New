T1 Income Tax Returns

Personal tax services and T1 income tax returns, done properly.

Your personal income tax accountant handles T1 returns for employees, self-employed people, retirees and students. We start from your slips — T4, T4A, T5, T3, T2202 and the rest — make sure every source of income is reported, and claim the deductions and credits you're actually entitled to, from RRSP contributions to tuition, medical, childcare and the home-office claim.

Whether this is your first year filing in Canada or your thirtieth, we get the return right and file it on time. The T1 deadline for most people is April 30; if you or your spouse are self-employed, you have until June 15 to file, but any balance owing is still due April 30. We'll flag what you owe early so there are no surprises.

Newcomers & Students

New to Canada or in school? Newcomer and student tax filing.

Newcomer tax filing

Your first Canadian return sets up benefits you don't want to miss — including the GST/HST credit and, if you have children, the Canada Child Benefit. We work out your residency start date, report income for the part of the year you were a resident, and file so those credit and benefit payments can begin.

  • • Residency start date and first-year filing
  • • Part-year resident returns
  • • GST/HST credit and Canada Child Benefit
  • • Foreign property and income reporting

Student tax filing

Even with little or no income, students should file — it triggers the GST/HST credit and builds tuition credits you can carry forward or transfer to a parent or spouse. We handle your T2202, any scholarship or part-time employment income, and returns for international students.

  • • Tuition credits from your T2202, carried forward or transferred
  • • Part-time and co-op employment income
  • • Scholarship, bursary and grant income
  • • International student returns
